# The Dirac Equation, the Heisenberg Cut, and the Measurement Problem — outline + draft abstract

*Working draft for a measurement-focused paper split off from `current_revision_DK_paper.md`.
Goal: the strong, defensible measurement core (Thesis B — three-stage registration + the
apparatus-dependent cut), with the Dirac/chiral structure included as **ontology of the

## The four disciplines this paper must hold (the whole game)

1. **Division of labor — stated in the first two pages.** The Dirac chiral structure supplies
   the *ontology* of the measured object (the internal clock; what is reversible; what a
   "definite outcome" is for a chiral phase). The detector's *ordinary electromagnetic*
   open-system physics supplies the *measurement engine* (capture, einselection of
   charge/position, dissipation, amplification). We do **not** claim the detector monitors
   the chiral scalar ψ̄ψ — §3.7 of the framework paper proves it does not.
2. **Relation to decoherence — concede its full domain, pick up where it is silent.**
   Environmental decoherence (the *unconditional* reduced dynamics) explains pointer-basis
   selection and the suppression of interference; we adopt that in full. It does **not**
   single out one outcome, and it does not describe the *conditional, apparatus-dependent*
   singling-out and irreversible stabilization of one alternative into a durable material
   record. That post-decoherence, detector-specific registration is this paper's subject.
   The Heisenberg cut is the reversible→irreversible boundary of that step.
3. **Spinor claim = binary-outcome structure, NOT entanglement-carrier.** The spin-½ spinor
   two-valuedness is *why* Stern–Gerlach and single-photon Bell outcomes are binary (two
   basins). Entanglement lives in the full composite state, not inside an L/R chiral pair —
   say so explicitly.
4. **Scope the "measurement problem."** Address the **cut + definiteness/registration**
   facet. Do **not** claim to derive the Born rule or supply the outcome-selection law —
   both stated as open. Naming the sub-problems and claiming only some reads as rigor.

## Draft abstract (~250 words)

Environmental decoherence explains why a measured system settles into a pointer basis and
loses interference, but the unconditional reduced state it produces is a stable improper
mixture: it does not single out one outcome, and it does not describe how one pointer
alternative becomes a durable, irreversible material record. We take decoherence's account in
full and ask what comes *after* it. Writing the Dirac equation in the chiral (Weyl) basis
exposes the fermion as two phase-clocks coupled by the mass term K = m, whose closed-regime
evolution is unitary precession with no attractor — the reversible, coherently recoverable
phase relationship on which decoherence's pointer selection acts. Measurement is then a
three-stage transition of this wavefunction — resonant capture, a provisional (still
reversible) commitment, and reservoir-powered registration — in which the detector's *ordinary
electromagnetic* open-system coupling, not the chiral coupling itself, supplies the measuring
interaction, while the chiral structure supplies the ontology of what is measured and what is
reversible. The Heisenberg cut is located at the reversible→irreversible boundary of this
transition, and is shown to be *apparatus-dependent* by working four canonical setups —
Stern–Gerlach, a Bell test with single-photon detectors, a hologram recorded on film, and a
cloud chamber — spanning binary pointer basins (fixed by spin-½ spinor structure) and
continuous pointer manifolds (accumulated exposure; particle trajectory). We are explicit
about which facet of the measurement problem this addresses — the cut and the
definiteness/registration of records — and about what it does not: it neither derives the
Born rule nor supplies an outcome-selection law, both stated as open.

## Section outline

**1. Introduction — which measurement problem.** Decompose the "measurement problem" into
the preferred-basis, definiteness/cut, and Born-probability sub-problems. Claim the
cut/definiteness facet; park Born and the selection law. State the two framings up front:
division of labor (§ above) and relation to decoherence.

**2. The object being measured: the Dirac chiral clock.** From framework §2.1–2.2 (+2.4):
chiral decomposition, closed-regime unitary precession with **no attractor** — this *is* the
reversible, coherently recoverable phase relationship. Ontology only; no preferred frame, no
gravity, no bulk cosmology. Establishes what "coherent evolution" and "reversible" mean
concretely.

**3. Where decoherence ends and this account begins.** Decoherence = unconditional pointer
selection + coherence loss (adopted). What it leaves open: the conditional, apparatus-
dependent formation of one irreversible record. Cite the conditional/unconditional
distinction honestly. The cut = the boundary; irreversibility is *effective* (information
dispersed beyond local recovery), not fundamental — global evolution stays unitary.

**4. Three stages of registration.** Capture (resonant coupling) → provisional commitment
(**reversible**, sub-threshold; latent-image specks decay, catch-and-reverse) → reservoir-
powered registration (**irreversible** amplification into a macroscopic record). Restate the
division of labor: the engine is electromagnetic. Stage 2 is a *provisional* commitment, NOT
an ontic outcome (this is the honest answer to the reviewer's Stage-2 concern).

**5. The reversible→irreversible boundary, formally.** From framework §3.2/§3.5 (Re W / Im W)
as the *effective-irreversibility* criterion — presented as a structural/effective-action
account, with the identifications labeled analogies unless a microscopic map is given.

**6. Four detectors, one structure, different pointer manifolds.** The core demonstration
that the cut is apparatus-dependent (table below). The spinor→binary vs continuous-manifold
spread is the payload.

**7. Synchronization: mechanism or metaphor?** Give ONE honest worked case — a phase
reduction H(sys+det+bath) → master equation → Adler/Kuramoto drift+noise → record — for the
single-photon avalanche (or the SG two-basin). Label the other three as *interpretation*:
entrainment into the apparatus's stable pointer manifold, of which the binary 0/π basin is a
special case. Do not use "synchronization" as a synonym for relaxation elsewhere.

**8. Scope and open problems.** Explicit parked items: Born not derived; outcome-selection
(background-field) law not supplied; the chiral-coupling→detector-pointer bridge is a
separate hypothesis (framework paper); no preferred-frame / gravity claims here.

## The four experiments (binary → continuous pointer manifold)

| Setup | Recorded variable | Pointer manifold | Registration mechanism | Spinor role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Stern–Gerlach** | spin up/down at screen | **binary** (2 basins, 0/π) | coherent spatial split → irreversible screen interaction | spin-½ → binary directly |
| **Bell, single-photon detectors** | click / no-click per detector | **binary** click + amplification | absorption → reservoir avalanche | binary outcome; entanglement in *full* state (flag) |
| **Hologram on film** | accumulated local intensity | **continuous** (exposure/dose field) | many weak capture–commit–register triplets; latent-image threshold | none direct — many-basin generalization |
| **Cloud chamber** | particle trajectory / deflection | **continuous** (position sequence) | repeated weak ionizing vertices, each nucleating a droplet (Mott) | none direct — repeated local commits |

Binary (top two, spinor-fixed) vs continuous (bottom two) is the demonstration that the cut
and the pointer geometry are set by the apparatus, not by a universal law.
