| [dispute-002](dispute-002-significance.md) | Is an imported-Born (Bohm-status) single-world interpretation a publishable foundations contribution? | 🟰 **standing dissent** — narrowed to a publish-bar standard; routed to the invited assessor |

This is the highest-leverage open question in the article — Fable 5 (Reviewer A) named it the single most important thing to settle. The five-round loop was **run on 2026-06-25** and resolved by a **commissioned, reproducible calculation** ([`dispute-001-basin-measure.py`](dispute-001-basin-measure.py)): the minimal two-outcome Adler model gives basin areas **50/50, flat in |α|²** and robust even to apparatus detuning, so the synchronization dynamics **do not derive Born** — consistency survives only by *importing* |α|² as a quantum-equilibrium measure (Bohm status; Reviewer A's "Horn 2"). A **standing dissent** remains on whether that imported-Born status is a foundations contribution (Fable 5, Gemini) or a non-result (GPT-5.5) — a significance judgment the calculation cannot settle. See the file for the round-by-round transcript and the honesty note on the loop's (facilitated, calculation-grounded) status.

**[dispute-002](dispute-002-significance.md)** is the *significance* standing-dissent spun off from dispute-001 — the value/venue judgment the calculation could not settle (is an honest, imported-Born, Bohm-status interpretation a publishable foundations contribution?). It cannot be settled by computation, so the loop instead **narrows** it: all three labs agree on the facts *and* on the required reframing ("interpretive synthesis, not a new mechanism"), leaving a single irreducible disagreement about the publish-bar standard. Recorded as a **standing dissent** and routed to the invited human assessor — plus the one lever that could retire it (develop the locking-threshold into a falsifiable prediction), now drafted as [dispute-002-retirement-path.md](dispute-002-retirement-path.md) and added to [`../experiment.md`](../experiment.md) as **T6**. **Part I (derivation) is executed** ([`dispute-002-partI-Keff.py`](dispute-002-partI-Keff.py)): K_eff = 4χ²n̄/κ derived from the transmon+readout master equation, SNIC critical exponent ½ confirmed — so dispute-001's "Adler not derived" is now discharged. Part II is now a **commit-ready experimental protocol** ([dispute-002-partII-protocol.md](dispute-002-partII-protocol.md)) — transmon + continuous readout + Rabi drive, four measurements with pulse sequences, ~1 device-week — an open call to an invited circuit-QED group. **(Reconciled 2026-06-30** with the second-revision Appendix D: K_eff and the cut location now sit in the manuscript and stand; outcome-registration moves to the conditional σ_z trajectory; the SNIC exponents become T6's *prediction* rather than a banked claim — see the [retirement-path reconciliation note](dispute-002-retirement-path.md).)

Together the two disputes demonstrate **both** terminal modes the protocol allows: dispute-001 *resolved by evidence* (a calculation), dispute-002 *standing dissent made legible* (a value judgment narrowed to its bedrock and routed). Neither was averaged into a fake middle.
