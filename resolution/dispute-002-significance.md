## Why this is a *standing-dissent* dispute, not an *open* one

"Open" would mean the parties have not yet engaged. They have — at length, from three labs — and the loop below shows the disagreement **shrinking to a single, sharp, irreducible point**. A standing dissent is the honest terminal state when that point is a *standard*, not a *fact*. The value of running the loop is locating exactly where the fact/agreement ends and the value-judgment begins.

## Round-by-round (≤5)

### Round 1 — the two camps
- **Against (GPT-5.5, C).** Recommendation **Reject**. "Decoherence, open-system attractors, quantum synchronization, preferred-frame hidden-variable theories, Bohmian equivariance problems, and gravitational-collapse analogies are known ingredients … not developed into a mathematically complete theory." - **For, conditionally (Fable 5, A; Gemini, B).** Recommendation **major revision** for a foundations venue. "The manuscript is a serious, scholarly contribution to the interpretation literature and is not crankish." Concern #3: "That is a legitimate contribution (unifying re-descriptions have value) … the novelty is an organizing identification and an ontological proposal, not a new mechanism."

➡ *Apparent gap:* reject vs major-revision — a two-step divergence on the recommendation scale.

### Round 2 — collapse the venue half of the gap
Both reviewers, in their own words, agree the manuscript is **not a results-journal paper**:
- **GPT-5.5:** "at best a speculative foundations/interpretation discussion venue, **not a physics journal requiring a worked dynamical theory or new testable prediction**."
- **Fable 5:** "Major revision, **for a foundations/interpretation venue** … For a results-oriented journal I would recommend **decline-as-out-of-scope**, and the author should not submit there."

➡ Much of the apparent gap is **venue-scoping artifact**: scored against a results journal, all three converge on *no*. Fix the venue to *foundations/interpretation* and ask the real question: does it clear **that** bar?

### Round 3 — collapse the factual half of the gap
Post–dispute-001, the parties agree on the **facts**: Born is not derived (basin measure 50/50, flat in |α|²); the dynamics do *registration/irreversibility*, not *statistics*; |α|² is imported as a quantum-equilibrium measure (Bohm status). No party now claims otherwise. So the dispute is **not about what the paper achieves technically.**

### Round 4 — the agreement is even deeper than it looks
The two camps agree on the **required reframing**, almost verbatim:
- **GPT-5.5 (C), Concern #8 fix:** "clearly state that the contribution is **an interpretive synthesis, not a new mechanism** unless derivations are supplied."
- **Fable 5 (A), Concern #3:** "the paper should say so without ambiguity: the novelty is **an organizing identification and an ontological proposal, not a new mechanism**."

So both reviewers agree the paper *is* an honestly-labeled interpretive synthesis, and agree it *should be relabeled as such*. The **entire residual dispute** is one threshold question:

> **Once honestly relabeled as "interpretive synthesis + single-world ontology + registration-as-locking, no new statistics," does that clear the publish bar for a foundations venue?**

- GPT-5.5's standard — a contribution needs a *new mechanism or a new testable prediction* — answers **no**.
- Fable 5 / Gemini's standard — a foundations contribution is judged on *coherence, honest delineation of the open, and freshness of framing* (no interpretation derives Born: not Bohm, not Everett, not GRW) — answers **yes, with revision**.

➡ This is a **standard/taste difference**, not a factual one. Argument cannot dissolve it; the loop has reached bedrock.

3. **The disagreement is purely a standard:** "new mechanism / new prediction required" (GPT-5.5 → reject) vs "coherence + honest delineation + fresh framing suffices for foundations" (Fable 5 / Gemini → major revision).
4. **It is partly retirable by work, not argument:** develop the locking-threshold into a falsifiable prediction and it crosses even the stricter bar.

This dispute does **not** route to apparatus directly — but the lever it identifies (the threshold prediction) is what *would* generate an apparatus test.
