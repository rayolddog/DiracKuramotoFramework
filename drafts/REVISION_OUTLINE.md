## 2. Two regimes of the chiral mass coupling
- 2.1 Chiral decomposition, Weyl basis; mass is the off-diagonal entry, K = m.
  Present modestly as a textbook structure under a new reading.
- 2.2 **Closed system = unitary normal-mode (Rabi) precession, NO attractor.**
  Promote Appendix F into the body: Madelung reduction, sine-in-amplitude /
  cosine-in-phase, no Lyapunov function, Liouville forbids attractors in
  closed/unitary dynamics. This is the rigorous core — lead with it.
- 2.3 **Open system = dissipative Adler/Kuramoto lock.** Needs a bath; this is
  where "synchronization" properly lives. The mass term is NOT a sync attractor.
- 2.4 Normal modes = de Broglie carrier + Zitterbewegung beat. **FIX the ZBW
  factor of 2:** the beat is the ±E splitting (2m at rest), not "temporal clock
  minus spatial clock" (which gives mc²/ℏ). Drop/repair the
  ω_t=E/ℏ vs ω_s=p²/mγℏ framing in §2.3/App B.

## 3. Measurement as resonant capture + bulk re-synchronization
- 3.1 Two stages: Stage 1 = resonant capture (weakly dissipative — the bound
  partner is never truly closed; laser / injection-locking model, with the
  Arnold-tongue capture range as the locking condition); Stage 2 = equilibration
  to the bulk.
- 3.2 **Reversible → irreversible boundary.** Stage 1 has a reversible window;
  Stage 2 is irreversible. Anchors: MRI spin echo (T2* refocusable vs T2/T1
  irreversible), Minev catch-and-reverse, Stern-Gerlach recombination.
- 3.3 Mass asymmetry (M ≫ m) → the small system conforms → one outcome per run.
  Careful: this delivers *definiteness*, not the Born *weights*.
- 3.4 Decoherence as *gaining* coherence with the bulk (the good reframing);
  consistent with einselection / quantum Darwinism.
- **COMPRESS:** the field-type taxonomy (weak/gluon/hadronic) to a short
  "scope across interactions" paragraph; keep Stage-1-conserves / Stage-2-
  dissipates energy accounting briefly.

## 4. The bulk reference (Φ_bulk)
- 4.1 What the bulk *is*: a faint (~ppm, per MRI) thermal bias, **not** an
  "entangled"/coherent macroscopic state. Maintained by the dominant local
  interaction — EM in any lab (B₀ in MRI), gravity only where EM is engineered
  away. **Drop "entangled bulk" language throughout.**
- 4.2 Absolute phase is gauge; **only differences are physical.** This rescues
  Φ_bulk from unfalsifiability — all observable content lives in phase
  *differences*, and the only novel observable difference is §6.
- 4.3 Order-parameter magnitude vs equilibration *rate* are independent (MRI
  point): a faint reference still drives fast, definite relaxation.
- 4.4 **FIX Γ_grav:** drop the unnormalized N² pair-sum (it's a dimensional
  artifact, not a rate). Confine gravity's coherence role to the EM-suppressed
  Penrose–Diósi regime. Do NOT claim gravity maintains everyday detector
  coherence.

## 5. Three regimes of capture (new unifying section)
- Table: **Bell detector** (strong/projective, one event; irreversibility =
  amplification, not the bare excitation) · **MRI** (isolated, deliberately-
  triggered Stage 2; B₀ = reference axis, gradient = spatial/SG-style encoder) ·
  **cloud chamber** (a *sequence* of weak partial Stage-1 captures each with a
  local Stage-2 nucleation — Mott collinearity, dE/dx — NOT one drawn-out
  Stage 2).
- Unifying axis: capture strength relative to the Arnold-tongue locking range
  (saturated → projective; near-edge → weak/repeated).

## 6. The distinctive prediction: linewidth-dependent gravitational Bell
- 6.1 The prediction: δφ_grav = ωΔΦ/(c²Δν); CHSH(Δν) = 2√2·exp(−δφ²/2).
  Standard QM/QED: linewidth-independent. Framework: CHSH degrades as linewidth
  narrows in a gravitational gradient.
- 6.2 **Resolve the §3.4-vs-§5.5 contradiction.** Does the bulk-phase mismatch
  accumulate coherently over τ_coh (observable, ~kHz linewidth) or reset per
  optical period (negligible)? Settle it with a clear physical argument; frame
  the prediction as a **falsification test of Φ_bulk**.
- 6.3 **Pre-empt Micius:** broad-linewidth SPDC makes δφ negligible, so existing
  satellite Bell tests do not falsify it; the test needs narrow-linewidth photons.
- 6.4 Parameter space: linewidth × altitude scan; honest sensitivity statement.
