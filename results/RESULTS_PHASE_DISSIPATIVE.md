# Does a sin 2φ term survive the dissipative limit? — RESULT

**Date:** 2026-06-13
**Code:** `code/phi_dissipative_check.py` → `code/phi_dissipative_check.png`
**Question (DISCRETIZATION_AS_SYNC_PAPER.md §3.2):** the continuous relative phase φ
of the chiral pair was claimed to "tend to 0 or π" by coupling. A first-harmonic
Adler term `dφ/dt = ω + K sin(Φ_bulk − φ)` is **monostable** (one basin). Does the
*dissipative* reduction of the chiral mass coupling instead carry a **second
harmonic** `sin 2φ` that stabilizes **both** 0 and π — a genuine binary?

## Setup

Chiral pair (ψ_L, ψ_R) = one qubit. Bloch vector `r = (⟨σx⟩,⟨σy⟩,⟨σz⟩)`; the
relative phase is the equatorial azimuth `φ = atan2(⟨σy⟩,⟨σx⟩)`:

- φ = 0 → in-phase `|L⟩+|R⟩` (+x pointer)
- φ = π → anti-phase `|L⟩−|R⟩` (−x pointer)

`φ → φ+π` is rotation by π about z, i.e. `σx → −σx`. A phase potential is period-π
(bistable 0/π candidate) **iff the dynamics is even under that flip**.

Closed Hamiltonian `H = −p σz + m σx` gives only precession (no attractor — §2.2).
Three dissipators compared (Lindblad `D[L]ρ = LρL† − ½{L†L,ρ}`, rate γ):

| model | jump operator | parity under φ→φ+π |
|---|---|---|
| **measure σx** (mass/interference channel) | `√γ σx` | **even** (quadratic in σx) |
| **polar T1** (relax toward one fixed axis +x) | amplitude damping → \|+x⟩ | odd |
| measure σz (dephasing in L/R basis, control) | `√γ σz` | — |

## Results (all numbers from the run)

**1. Phase flow on the equator (H=0, rate=1):**

| model | harmonics of dφ/dt | stable fixed pts | verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **measure σx** | sin φ = 0.000, **sin 2φ = −1.000** | **0° and 180°** | **pure 2nd harmonic → BISTABLE** |
| polar T1 | sin φ = −1.000, sin 2φ = +0.250 | 0° only | 1st harmonic → MONOSTABLE |
| measure σz | sin φ = 0, sin 2φ = 0 | none (flow ≡ 0) | pure dephasing → phase destroyed |

The measurement dissipator gives **exactly** `dφ/dt = −γ sin 2φ` — a clean second
harmonic, stable at **both** 0 and π, unstable at ±π/2. The effective potential is
`V(φ) = −½cos 2φ`, a **double well** at 0 and π (vs the polar case's single well
`−cos φ`). So **yes, the sin 2φ term survives — it is exact, not perturbative.**

**2. Second-harmonic Adler / locking tongue:** with residual precession Ω in the
measured plane, `dφ/dt = Ω − γ sin 2φ`:

| Ω/γ | result | stable phases |
|---|---|---|
| 0.00 | LOCKED, 2 basins | 0°, 180° |
| 0.50 | LOCKED, 2 basins | 15°, 195° |
| 0.90 | LOCKED, 2 basins | 32°, 212° |
| 1.00 | UNLOCKED — phase winds | — |
| 1.50 | UNLOCKED — phase winds | — |

The **bistable binary survives whenever |Ω| < γ** (measurement dominates the
residual precession). The two basins sit symmetrically about 0 and π and merge/melt
at the threshold |Ω| = γ — an Arnold tongue **for a binary**.

**3. Quantum trajectories** (continuous σx measurement, 600 runs from |L⟩, γ=4):
collapse onto the two pointer phases — 49.5% → φ=0, 50.5% → φ=π, mean |⟨σx⟩| = 1.000.
The relative phase is driven to **0 or π** every run. (The ~50/50 split is the Born
weight of |L⟩ **assumed by the unraveling**, not derived — see caveats.)

## Verdict: conditional YES

A `sin 2φ` term **does** survive the dissipative limit and **does** stabilize both 0
and π — **provided Stage 2 is a *measurement* of the interference (mass) channel**
(Hermitian dissipator `D[σx]`, pointer states = σx eigenstates), **not polar
relaxation toward a fixed axis**. The polar case gives `−sin φ` and one basin (the
§3.2 monostability worry); the measurement case gives `−sin 2φ` and the binary. The
discriminator is exactly the apolar/even structure: `D[σx]` is quadratic in σx.

This is the user's "0 or π by coupling" intuition **vindicated with a precise
condition** — and it is grounded standard physics: it is the pointer-state
(einselection) structure of continuous measurement of a two-outcome observable. The
framework's *addition* is the identification — the Stage-2 measured observable is
the chiral interference σx, so the two pointers ARE the in-phase / anti-phase
relative-phase configurations.

## Caveats (what it does NOT buy)

1. **Internal-consistency item flagged in §3.2:** the companion's §2.3 Adler
   reduction is written first-harmonic, `ω + K sin(Φ_bulk − φ)` = injection-locking
   to a *biased (polar)* reference = the **monostable** case. The binary requires
   the *apolar* measurement dissipator `D[σx]`, a **different bath coupling**. §2.3
   as written does not itself produce the binary; this should be reconciled.
2. **Cardinality not earned.** It is "2" because σx (a qubit/spin-½ observable) has
   two eigenvalues — the chiral two-level structure. The count is representation
   theory again (consistent with §3.2), not generated by the locking.
3. **Born weights not earned.** The trajectory split is the Born weight built into
   the stochastic unraveling, not an independent derivation. The weights remain the
   open §6.1 problem.
4. **No new physics.** This is standard quantum-measurement / pointer-state theory
   in a two-level system; the contribution is a consistent *identification* and a
   sharp falsifiable condition (|Ω| < γ), the same honest status as the rest of the
   program.
